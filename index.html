Frequently Asked Questions

How often should I water my indoor plants?

+

Most indoor plants prefer soil that is moist but not waterlogged. Generally, water when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch. This typically means watering once a week, but it varies by plant type and humidity. Succulents need less frequent watering, while tropical plants may need more.

What plants are best for beginners?

+

Some excellent beginner-friendly plants include Pothos, Snake Plant, ZZ Plant, Spider Plant, and Succulents. These plants are forgiving, require minimal care, tolerate low light, and can handle inconsistent watering. They're perfect for building confidence before moving to more demanding plants.

How much sunlight do most plants need?

+

Light requirements vary by plant. Most indoor plants prefer bright, indirect light (3-6 hours daily). Succulents and cacti need more direct sunlight (6-8 hours), while shade-tolerant plants like Pothos can survive in low light. Position plants near a window but out of direct rays to prevent leaf burn.
